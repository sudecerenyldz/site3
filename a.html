House Viewing Tips

House Viewing Tips

DRIVE BY

First impressions shouldn't be underestimated. Even before the viewing, you should go and see the property from the outside.

BE PREPARED

Make a list of all the things that are important to you and any questions you want to ask.

TAKE A FRIEND

Never go to a viewing alone. Either take a partner, friend or attend with an agent.

CHECK THE OUTSIDE

Are there any signs of damp like peeling paint or tide marks on the walls?

BE THOROUGH

Check all fixed cupboards, especially under the sink. No one will blame you for being nosy.

CHECK THE PLUMBING

How long does it take the hot water to come through? Does the air conditioning work?

CHECK THE BILLS

Ask to see utility and council tax bills so you know what costs to expect.

WHAT'S INCLUDED?

Ask what fixtures and fittings the owners will be leaving behind.

WHAT WORK HAS BEEN DONE?

Ask about modifications and request copies of receipts and habitation certificates.

LOOK OUT FOR DAMP

Keep an eye out for peeling paint, stains on walls, and steamed up windows.
